Mars information

  • discovery: Known by the ancient Romans and Greeks and visible to the naked eye.
  • Named after: Roman god of war.
  • Diameter: 6,787km.
  • Orbit: 687 Earth days.
  • Date: Just over one Earth day (24 hours, 37 minutes).

Here are the details about Mars

Mars researchers are focusing on two sensors on Earth and the planet orbiting it to better understand the source methane on the Red Planet. The fourth planet from the Sun, Mars is a rocky and cold planet. Dirt is an iron oxide, which is present in abundance on the planet's surface, causing its surface to appear with a characteristic red color. The planet Mars shares many similarities with Earth: its surface is rocky, has mountains and valleys, and a storm system that ranges from cyclonic storms - like dust-carrying winds - to hurricanes. Dust storms engulf the planet. Dust covers the surface of Mars and the planet Mars is filled with frozen water. Scientists think that the planet Mars will be flooded with liquid water as soon as the temperature warms up, although it is currently a cold and desert-like planet. Mars' atmosphere is too thin for liquid water to exist on the planet's surface for any length of time. Scientists think the ancient planet Mars had the conditions for life, and hope that signs of life in the past - even in biology in the present - can survive on the red planet.
