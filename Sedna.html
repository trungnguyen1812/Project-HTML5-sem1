Sedna information

  • Discovery: was discovered by Michael Brown (Caltech), Chad Trujillo (Gemini Observatory) and David Rabinowitz (Yale University) on November 14, 2003.
  • Named after: The name of the Eskimo sea goddess is Sedna, who lives at the bottom of the cold Arctic Ocean. Before it was officially named, its codename was 2003 VB12.
  • Diameter: From 1200 to 1600 km.
  • Orbit: Is only estimated at about 10.5 to 12 thousand Earth years.
  • Date:...

Here are the details about Sedna

Sedna is a celestial body located very far away in the solar system, beyond the orbit of Neptune and can be classified as a dwarf planet. Currently Sedna is 88 astronomical units (AU) from the sun, 3 times farther than Neptune. In its orbit, Sedna is often at a distance greater than anyone can consider a recognized dwarf planet.
